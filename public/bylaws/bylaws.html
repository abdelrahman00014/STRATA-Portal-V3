STRATA SCHEME BYLAWS

1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 In these by-laws, unless the context otherwise requires:

  • "building" means the building and all improvements on the parcel;
  • "common property" means so much of the parcel as is not comprised in any lot;
  • "Council" means the council of the body corporate;
  • "lot" means a lot in the scheme;
  • "owner" means the registered proprietor for the time being of a lot;
  • "parcel" means the land subdivided by the scheme;
  • "proprietor" includes the owner for the time being of a lot and a mortgagee in possession;

1.2 Words and expressions defined in the Strata Titles Act have the same meaning in these by-laws.

2. DUTIES OF BODY CORPORATE

2.1 The duties of the body corporate include:

  1. controlling, managing and administering the common property;
  2. properly maintaining and keeping in a state of good and serviceable repair the common property and personal property vested in the body corporate;
  3. maintaining lawns and garden areas within the common property;
  4. maintaining and repairing (including renewals where necessary) pipes, cables, ducts and conduits for the time being existing on the parcel and capable of being used in connection with the enjoyment of more than one lot or the common property;

3. POWERS OF BODY CORPORATE

3.1 The powers of the body corporate include:

  1. purchasing, hiring or otherwise acquiring personal property;
  2. entering into agreements for the provision of amenities or services to any part of the parcel;
  3. instituting, prosecuting, defending, discontinuing, or settling legal proceedings relating to the body corporate;

4. NOISE AND BEHAVIOR

4.1 A proprietor or occupier shall not:

  1. make or permit any noise or behavior likely to interfere with the peaceful enjoyment of the proprietor or occupier of another lot or of any person lawfully using common property;
  2. use language or behave in a manner likely to cause offense or embarrassment to the proprietor or occupier of another lot or to any person lawfully using common property;
  3. obstruct the lawful use of common property by any person.

4.2 After 10:00 p.m. and before 8:00 a.m., a proprietor or occupier shall keep noise from radios, televisions, music and machinery at a level that does not interfere with the peaceful enjoyment of other proprietors and occupiers.

5. FUNCTIONS AND EVENTS

5.1 A proprietor or occupier who organizes functions or events within their lot must:

  1. notify neighbors at least 48 hours in advance of any function that may cause noise or disturbance;
  2. ensure that all guests behave in a manner that does not disturb others;
  3. ensure that the function concludes by 11:00 p.m. on weekdays and 12:00 a.m. on weekends.

6. DAMAGE TO COMMON PROPERTY

6.1 A proprietor or occupier shall not mark, paint, drive nails or screws or the like into, or otherwise damage or deface, any structure that forms part of the common property without the approval in writing of the body corporate.

6.2 An approval given by the body corporate under by-law 6.1 cannot authorize any additions to the common property.

6.3 This by-law does not prevent a proprietor or person authorized by him from installing:

  1. any locking or other safety device for protection of the proprietor's lot against intruders; or
  2. any screen or other device to prevent entry of animals or insects on the lot.

7. APPEARANCE OF LOT

7.1 A proprietor or occupier shall not, except with the written approval of the body corporate, maintain within the lot anything visible from outside the lot that, viewed from outside the lot, is not in keeping with the appearance of the rest of the building.

7.2 This includes:

  1. hanging laundry, towels, bedding or other articles on any part of the lot in a way visible from outside;
  2. installing window coverings (such as curtains, blinds, or shutters) that are not neutral in color when viewed from outside;
  3. placing or displaying on the exterior of the lot any sign, advertisement, or notice.

8. KEEPING OF ANIMALS

8.1 A proprietor or occupier shall not, without the written approval of the body corporate, keep any animal on the lot or the common property.

8.2 The body corporate shall not unreasonably withhold approval of the keeping of an animal.

8.3 A proprietor or occupier who keeps an animal on a lot or the common property:

  1. must ensure that the animal is supervised and under effective control when on the common property;
  2. must promptly clean up after the animal if it defecates or urinates on common property;
  3. is liable for damage to common property caused by the animal;
  4. must ensure the animal does not cause a nuisance to other proprietors or occupiers.

9. PET REGISTRATION

9.1 All pets must be registered with the body corporate. Registration requires:

  1. Providing details of the type, breed, and size of the pet;
  2. Providing evidence of current vaccinations;
  3. Paying any applicable pet registration fee as determined by the body corporate.

9.2 The body corporate may revoke approval for keeping a pet if:

  1. The pet causes a nuisance to other proprietors or occupiers after the owner has been given notice to address the issue;
  2. The pet poses a danger to residents or visitors;
  3. The proprietor or occupier fails to comply with any conditions imposed by the body corporate for keeping the pet.
